# Articles
The Articles section is the public explanatory field of Configism and Configurational Logic.

These texts do not replace the **Core** and do not present the canon directly. The articles show how the same logical problems appear across different fields of reality.

Each article takes one concrete phenomenon and reveals its internal structure: what enters the system, what receives status, what gains weight, what changes the vector, where connection becomes defined, where it is preserved, and where it breaks.

For a first-time reader, this section should be read not as a blog and not as a set of separate essays, but as a chain of conceptual bridges. Some articles explain the basic logic of interaction and fixation. Other articles show how living and artificial systems work with signal, status, weight, choice, and action. Part of the section examines how AI systems lose coherence, shift mode, or redistribute internal power. Other articles work with language, meaning, sophistry, and the collapse of a shared field of understanding.

The purpose of this section is to make Configism and Configurational Logic accessible through real problems before entering the stricter structure of the **Core**.

This section is written for those who want to understand how complex systems preserve or lose defined connection before the visible result appears.
